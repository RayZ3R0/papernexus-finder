Procedure
	
Step 1	 Transfer 2.00 g of 2-hydroxybenzoic acid into a dry, pear-shaped flask.   
Add 4.0 cm3 of ethanoic anhydride, followed by 5 drops of 
concentrated phosphoric acid.
	
Step 2	 Fit the flask with a reflux condenser and heat it on a hot water bath for about 
five minutes.
	
Step 3	 After allowing the reaction mixture to cool, add 2 cm3 of water down the 
condenser.  This hydrolyses excess ethanoic anhydride.
	
Step 4	 When the vigorous reaction has ended, pour the mixture into 40 cm3 of  
cold water in a 100 cm3 beaker in an ice-water bath.  Solid aspirin forms.
	
Step 5	 Filter the mixture under reduced pressure.  Wash the solid aspirin with a little 
cold distilled water.
	
Step 6	 Recrystallise the product using distilled water and dry it.
